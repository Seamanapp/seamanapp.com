False Alert Cancellation Card

The card from the GMDSS video — keep it on your phone, print it for the radio console.

Sent a distress alert by accident? Do NOT just switch the set off — rescue services would keep searching for a ship in danger. Cancel it at once, by voice, on the right frequency. If the alert actually went out, also tell the RCC / MRCC that it was false.

🖨 Print / Save as PDF

Say this — slowly, and again if needed

ALL STATIONS, ALL STATIONS, ALL STATIONS
THIS IS — [ship's name], [call sign], MMSI (9 digits)
POSITION — [latitude and longitude]
CANCEL MY DISTRESS ALERT OF [time UTC], [date]
MASTER — [ship's name], [call sign], MMSI] — [time UTC]

Cancel on the frequency that matches the alert you sent

VHF DSC — Channel 70

Reset the DSC controller, then broadcast the cancellation by voice on VHF Channel 16.

MF DSC — 2187.5 kHz

Reset the controller, then cancel by voice on 2182 kHz.

HF DSC

Reset the controller, then cancel by voice on the radiotelephone distress frequency of the SAME HF band you alerted on — and say which band (e.g. 8 MHz → 8291 kHz).

Inmarsat-C / Fleet (satellite)

There is no on-air cancel — contact the LES / RCC you alerted, by any means, and tell them the alert was false and no assistance is required.

406 MHz EPIRB

Switch the EPIRB OFF, then contact the nearest RCC / MRCC by any working means and give the beacon's hex ID, the ship's name and position, and "no assistance required."

Then, every time

  • Report the false alert as your flag State and company SMS require, and log it.
  • Guard the distress button and brief the crew so it can't happen again.

GUIDANCE only — a memory aid, not the rulebook. It does not replace your GMDSS operator's certificate (GOC / ROC), the equipment's own manuals, or the Admiralty List of Radio Signals (ALRS). Follow IMO MSC.1/Circ.1210 and your company SMS: the certificated radio operator transmits, the Master decides.
